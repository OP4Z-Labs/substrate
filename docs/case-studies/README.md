## Contributing a case study

Three slots are open at v1.0. To claim one:

1. Open an issue titled `[case-study] <your-project>` describing:
   - What you use cadence for.
   - Which audits / standards / commands you rely on most.
   - The size of the codebase / team.
2. Land a PR copying one of the template files (`case-study-02.md`
   through `case-study-05.md`) with your content.
3. Cadence maintainers review for accuracy, not for content
   judgement — you describe your experience honestly; we don't
   second-guess.

Length target: 600-1500 words per case study. Include:

- Background (team size, codebase, stack).
- What problem cadence solves for you.
- Which specific cadence features you depend on.
- Anything that bit you / required a workaround.
- What you'd want next.

Bad case studies are pure marketing. Good case studies are "here's
what works, here's what didn't, here's what we'd change."
